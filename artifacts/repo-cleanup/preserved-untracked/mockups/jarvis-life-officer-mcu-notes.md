# JARVIS New Design Direction

## Grounding from the repo vision

From the current JARVIS canon:

- JARVIS is a conversation-first Life Operating Officer.
- Conversation is the operating system.
- JARVIS should turn objectives into missions.
- JARVIS should do useful work between conversations.
- Frontend should surface stewardship, not become a generic dashboard.

## Grounding from MCU JARVIS ideas

The best reusable MCU traits are not the noisy HUD layers themselves.

They are:

- calm competence
- context awareness
- goal awareness
- bringing forward the right display at the right moment
- quiet execution in the background
- making the human feel supported rather than overloaded

## Design translation

This new design treats JARVIS as:

- a strategic presence
- a mission steward
- an authority-aware operating layer
- a continuity engine that explains what changed while you were away

## Core layout decisions

- Replace “dashboard home” with a mission-first hero.
- Make the first interaction “state an objective” rather than “pick a tab.”
- Turn updates into a guided mission feed with rationale and next actions.
- Turn approvals into an authority spine rather than scattered indicators.
- Show always-on work as a continuity timeline, not as ambient visual noise.

## Visual language

- Keep Glass as the canonical material system.
- Borrow MCU tone: dark, quiet, premium, composed, high-trust.
- Avoid copying cinematic HUD clutter literally.
- Use luminous cyan as a guidance accent, not as a decoration layer everywhere.
- Motion should feel like systems stabilizing into place, not screens performing.
