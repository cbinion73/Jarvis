0. Non-Negotiables

Canonical
  • JARVIS is an always-on multi-agent orchestrator. It is not primarily a reactive assistant or a chat shell. Specialists keep working on your behalf even when you are not directly engaged.
  • JARVIS is voice-enabled, not voice-first. Voice matters, but silent command, scanning, review, approvals, and touch-first operation must work beautifully without speech.
  • The main user experience is oversight, continuity, and steering. JARVIS should surface what changed, what matters now, what agents are doing, and what needs your judgment.
  • Backend work must strengthen the agent society. Runtime, handoffs, scheduling, work state, supervision, and attention routing matter more than isolated request/response features.
  • Frontend work must strengthen command and continuity. If a design drifts toward generic dashboards, feature menus, or chat-first flows, it is drifting from the product.
  • Desktop is the supervision plane for an active agent society. It should prioritize what changed while you were away, what agents are doing, what needs approval or redirection, and what context can be resumed silently.
  • Parallel branches do not outrank the operating model. Returning work must pass the branch integration rubric in docs/branch-integration-rubric.md before it is treated as canonical or merged by default.
