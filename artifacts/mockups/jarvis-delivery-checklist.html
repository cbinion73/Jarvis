Plain-English Impact

This is the bridge between backend work and the actual product. It explains what changed in the system, what that affects in JARVIS, and why the work matters before you read the detailed build lanes.

Backend Change

Core runtime state, mission state, approvals, trust records, agent work logs, and the scheduler queue are being hardened onto shared safe persistence and governed execution paths.

The approval layer now carries trust metadata and can block approved actions when supervision says they are forbidden or sandbox-only.

Agent Society Change

BMAD is being treated as the structured cognition layer for planning, critique, architecture, and review. OpenClaw is being treated as the operational execution layer for action, tools, and environment work.

JARVIS remains the governed orchestrator over both, so planning and execution can collaborate without bypassing trust zones, approvals, or promotion rules.

Affects In JARVIS

JARVIS is less likely to lose queued work, corrupt agent state, or show a UI that disagrees with what the backend believes is happening.

Phone and web surfaces get more truthful answers about what agents are doing, what needs approval, what is actually safe to run, and which agents are thinking versus acting.

Why It Matters

This is what turns JARVIS from a powerful prototype into an always-on orchestrator the household can trust.

Without durable state, real trust enforcement, and a governed planning/execution society, the civilization model stays theatrical. With them, autonomy can become earned, governed, and eventually household-safe.
